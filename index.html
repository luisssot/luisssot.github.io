Articles

  • On the Axiomatic Formulation in Empirical Sciences
    The text compares axiomatic formulations in mathematics and physics, highlighting that mathematics relies on logical consistency, while physics requires experimental verification. Using Callen’s thermodynamic postulates as an example, it points out that scale non-homogeneity can cause inconsistencies. These fundamental differences affect how theories are constructed and validated in each discipline, making them crucial for research and development. [Article] [Certificate]
  • On the Relationship Between Two Approaches to Internal Losses in a Finite-Time Joule-Brayton Cycle
    The work compares two approaches for modeling internal irreversibilities in a Joule-Brayton cycle, representative of gas turbines. One approach considers fluid friction with mechanical components and includes an “agglomeration” parameter derived from Clausius' inequality. The other, proposed by Gordon and Huleihil, uses parameters that quantify deviations from the ideal adiabatic branches. Both models successfully reproduce the ripple-shaped power-efficiency curves characteristic of real-world machines. [Article] [Certificate]
  • Electrodynamics of Rayleigh Scattering: Modeling of Sky Color and its Influence on Oceanic Appearance
    An electrodynamic study of Rayleigh scattering is presented, focusing on the coloration of the sky and the ocean. Based on Maxwell's equations, the interaction of solar radiation with atmospheric and oceanic molecules is modeled in the $\lambda \gg d$ regime, recovering the $\lambda^{-4}$ spectral dependence. In the atmosphere, blue light is scattered more intensely, while in the ocean absorption and particulate scattering are included. The model explains both the blue tones of open seas and the green hues of coastal waters, providing a unified optical description of sky and ocean. [Article] [Certificate]
